## Learnings

### Day 1 (2025-10-26)

**User Experience Learnings:**
1. **Navigation is invisible until it's missing** - I felt trapped without back button
2. **Endings need closure** - Dead ends feel like bugs, not story endings
3. **Context matters** - Story title, progress, where am I?
4. **Feedback is essential** - Loading states, button responses, confirmation
5. **Restart is expected** - I want to explore different choices

## My Testing Methodology

### How I Test:

1. **Fresh eyes** - Test on my phone at home, not in office
2. **Real scenarios** - "I want to read a fantasy story" not "test the reader"
3. **Honest reactions** - If I'm confused, I say it
4. **Edge interview** - He asks specific questions, I answer honestly
5. **Story creation** - I create stories to test editor

### What I Look For:

**Not technical bugs** (Edge finds those):
- Confusion points
- Frustration moments
- Delightful surprises
- Emotional impact
- Flow disruptions

### My "H√§?"-Moment System:

When I'm confused, I document:
1. **What I expected** - "I thought clicking here would..."
2. **What happened** - "But instead..."
3. **How I felt** - "Which made me feel..."

**This helps Edge translate to bugs.**
